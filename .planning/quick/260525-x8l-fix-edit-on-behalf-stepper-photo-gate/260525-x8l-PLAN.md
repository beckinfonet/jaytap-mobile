<objective>
Close the photo-gate backdoor in the moderator "Edit on behalf" stepper. Today, a mod/admin reviewing a pending listing with zero photos can tap "Edit on behalf", walk through Steps 1-5, and at Step 6 the "Save and approve" / "Сохранить и одобрить" button is enabled — letting them dispatch PUT /moderation/listings/:id with the user perception that the listing is now live (the button label says "approve"). Although the backend PUT route is field-only (per quick 260511-cog) and the real /approve endpoint still rejects MEDIA_REQUIRED (per quick 260514-rk1), the misleading button label combined with the missing client-side guardrail violates the parity established by 260514-rk1: every moderator entry point must surface the photo-required guidance before the mod commits the action.

Purpose: Defense-in-depth UX parity with PropertyDetailsScreen's NeedsMediaBanner pattern. Single trust boundary stays on the backend (/approve MEDIA_REQUIRED); client gate is guidance only.

Output: Step 6 submit button disabled + inline warning row when mode === 'edit-mod' AND initialListing has zero photos. Reuses existing i18n keys — no new strings, no new dependencies, no backend change.
</objective>
